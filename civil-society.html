    Konsèy Prezidansyèl Tranzisyonèl (CPT) ak Sosyete Sivil

    Konpozisyon CPT

    Nan mas 2024, Ayiti te etabli yon Konsèy Prezidansyèl Tranzisyonèl ak 9 manm:

    • 7 manm votyan: Reprezante pati politik ak sektè sosyete sivil
    • 2 obsèvatè san dwa vòt: Patisipe nan diskisyon men pa vote

    Patisipasyon Sosyete Sivil

    Sosyete sivil te patisipe nan kreyasyon CPT la:

    • Negosyasyon akò politik ki te mennen nan CPT
    • Reprezantasyon kèk sektè nan Konsèy la
    • Monitè aksyon CPT la
    • Mande transpare ak responsabilite

    Defi ak Kritik

    Sosyete sivil te kritike CPT pou:

    • Mank transpare nan desizyon
    • Konfli enterè ant manm yo
    • Pa mande sitwayen òdinè yo opinyon
    • Repete eritye politik tradisyonèl

    Pwogram Ranfòsman Sosyete Sivil

    USAID Civil Society Strengthening Program

    USAID ap sipòte pwogram pou ranfòse kapasite sosyete sivil Ayisyen:

    • Fòmasyon sou jesyon òganizasyonèl
    • Sipò finansye pou pwojè kominotè
    • Ranfòsman kapasite defans ak plede
    • Netwòking ant òganizasyon

    FEF (Fonds Egalité Femmes) 2025-2026

    Anbasad Frans te lanse FEF pou sipòte òganizasyon k ap travay sou dwa fanm:

    • Finanse pwojè egalite jand
    • Sipò lit kont vyolans baze sou sèks
    • Responsabilizasyon ekonomik fanm
    • Patisipasyon politik fanm

    Lòt Patnè Entènasyonal

    • Inyon Ewopeyèn: Sipò dwa moun ak demokrasi
    • Nasyon Zini: Pwogram devlopman ak dwa sivil
    • Kanadà: Sipò sosyete sivil ak bòn gouvènans
    • Òganizasyon entènasyonal ONG: Kolaborasyon ak gwoup lokal

    Defi Sosyete Sivil ann Ayiti

    Ensèkirite

    Vyolans gang yo limite kapasite òganizasyon yo pou opere:

    • Militanw dwa moun anba menas
    • Biwo ONG fèmen akoz vyolans
    • Difisil pou rive jwenn kominote nan zòn andanje
    • Aktivis oblije kite peyi a

    Mank Resous

    Anpil òganizasyon depann de finansman etranje:

    • Mank sibi lokalman
    • Depandans sou donasyon entènasyonal
    • Difikilte pou peye pèsonèl kalifye
    • Ekipman ak enfrastrikti limite

    Kriz Politik

    Enstabilite politik kreye anviwònman difisil:

    • Gouvènman ki chanje souvan pa bay kontinite
    • Priorite politik pa reflete bezwen sosyete sivil
    • Repression kont kritik gouvènman
    • Mank espas pou dyalòg reyèl

    Divizyon Entèn

    Sosyete sivil pa toujou ini:

    • Konpetisyon pou finansman kreye rivalite
    • Diferans ideolojik separe òganizasyon
    • Mank kòdinasyon limit enpak
    • Kèk òganizasyon tro pre de politik

    Mank Konfyans Piblik

    Apre deseni koripsyon, kèk sitwayen pa fè ONG konfyans:

    • Pèsepsyon ke ONG ap fè biznis
    • Akizasyon move jesyon oswa koripsyon
    • Santi òganizasyon twò lwen de reyalite pèp la
    • Defi pou mobilize patisipasyon mas

    Enpak Sosyete Sivil sou Chanjman Sosyal

    Mouvman PetroChallenge (2018-2019)

    Sosyete sivil te jwe wòl santral nan mobilizasyon kont koripsyon:

    • Pibliye rapò UCREF sou detounman $4 milya
    • Òganize manifestasyon ak grèv jeneral
    • Itilize hashtag #KotKòbPetwoKaribea pou mobilize
    • Mete presyon pou jistis (malgre echèk jiska kounye a)

    Defans Dwa Tè Peyizan

    Òganizasyon peyizan te batay kont agribiznis ki ap pran tè yo:

    • Mouvman Tèt Kole lit pou souverènte alimantè
    • MPNKP travay nan 10 depatman pou repopilasyon kochon ak agroecology
    • OPODNE mobilize 56 kominote nan Nòdès
    • Rezistans kont pwojè ki deplase peyizan

    Avanse Dwa Fanm

    Mouvman fanm Ayisyen te genyen viktwa enpòtan:

    • Kriminalizasyon vyòl nan Kòd Penal 2005
    • Lwa sou Patnè nan Fè 2013
    • Kota 30% fanm nan pozisyon elektif (malgre pa toujou aplike)
    • Kreye ministè ak enstitisyon pou dwa fanm

    Monitè Eleksyon

    Sosyete sivil jwe yon wòl kle nan eleksyon:

    • Obsèvasyon elektoral endepandan
    • Edikasyon votè
    • Rapòte fwòd ak iregilarite
    • Plede pou refòm elektoral

    Kijan Angaje nan Sosyete Sivil

    1. Jwenn yon Òganizasyon

    Chèche gwoup ki travay sou kesyon ki enpòtan pou ou:

    • Dwa moun, jistis, transpare
    • Dwa fanm, egalite jand
    • Anviwònman, agrikilti viab
    • Edikasyon, sante, devlopman

    2. Volontè Tan ou

    • Patisipe nan aktivite òganizasyon
    • Ofri konpetans ou (desen, teknoloji, jesyon)
    • Ede nan kanpay enfòmasyon
    • Sipòte lojistik pou evènman

    3. Sipò Finansyèman

    • Fè don menm ti kòb ka ede
    • Vin manm òganizasyon
    • Achte pwodui sosyal (artiza, koperativ)
    • Patisipe nan aktivite levé fon

    4. Pataje Enfòmasyon

    • Swiv òganizasyon sou medya sosyal
    • Pataje rapò ak enfòmasyon enpòtan
    • Pale ak lòt moun sou travay òganizasyon yo
    • Konbat dezenfòmasyon

    5. Kreye Pwòp Òganizasyon Ou

    Si pa gen òganizasyon k ap travay sou pwoblèm ou wè:

    • Rasanble lòt moun ki pataje vizyon ou
    • Defini misyon ak objektif klè
    • Kòmanse ak aksyon ti echèl nan kominote ou
    • Chèche sipò ak konseye lòt òganizasyon

    Pwoteksyon Militanw Sosyete Sivil

    Risk yo Konfwonte

    Militanw dwa moun ak jounalis ann Ayiti souvan anba menas:

    • Entimidason ak atak fizik
    • Menas sou lavi yo
    • Poursuit legal abiziv
    • Kannpay difamasyon

    Mezi Pwoteksyon

    • Netwòk solidarite ant òganizasyon
    • Alet rapid lè militanw anba menas
    • Atansyon entènasyonal pou ka seriz
    • Sipò legal ak sikolojik

    Bezwen pou yon Lwa

    Ayiti bezwen yon lwa pou pwoteje defansè dwa moun ki ta:

    • Rekonèt wòl enpòtan militanw
    • Garanti sekirite yo
    • Sanksyone moun ki atake yo
    • Pwoteje denonsyatè koripsyon

    Avni Sosyete Sivil ann Ayiti

    Opòtinite

    • Teknoloji: Medya sosyal ak platfòm dijital bay nouvo zouti pou mobilizasyon
    • Dyaspora: Ayisyen aletranje ka sipòte travay òganizasyon lokal
    • Jèn: Nouvo jenerasyon militanw ak ide inovatè
    • Rezilians: Istwa rich rezistans ak solidarite

    Defi Kontinye

    • Bezwen pou plis sibi lokal ak mwens depandans etranje
    • Ranfòse kòdinasyon ant òganizasyon
    • Pwofi nan kominote riral ak zòn manzinen
    • Rebati konfyans ant òganizasyon ak mas popilè

    Priyorite pou Lavan

    • Ranfòse kapasite òganizasyon lokal
    • Pwoteje militanw dwa moun
    • Pwomote transpare nan sektè sosyete sivil
    • Ankouraje patisipasyon sitwayen mas
    • Bati alianz solid pou chanjman sosyal

    Konklizyon

    Sosyete sivil se espindòsal yon demokrasi ki fonksyone. Ann Ayiti, malgre defi enormès, òganizasyon sosyete sivil kontinye defann dwa sitwayen, mande responsabilite gouvènman, ak travay pou yon sosyete pi jis.

    Chak sitwayen ka kontribye nan sosyete sivil – kit se kòm manm yon òganizasyon, volontè, donasyon, oswa senpleman pataje enfòmasyon ak defann prensip demokratik.

    Sosyete sivil se vwa pèp la. Lè nou òganize ansanm, nou pi fò pase gouvènman ki pa rann kont, kriminèl ki abize pouvwa, ak sistèm ki eksplwate nou.

    What is Civil Society?

    Civil society is all the organizations, groups, and associations that exist between government and private families. It is the space where citizens gather to engage in public issues, defend their interests, and work for the common good.

    Civil society includes:

    • NGOs (Non-Governmental Organizations): Groups working on development, human rights, environment
    • Unions: Organizations defending workers' rights
    • Professional Associations: Groups of doctors, lawyers, journalists, agronomists
    • Religious Groups: Churches, religious confederations, faith-based organizations
    • Social Movements: Groups fighting for social justice, women's rights, environment
    • Community Organizations: Neighborhood committees, village groups, local associations

    Role of Civil Society in Democracy

    1. Government Accountability

    Civil society watches government and demands responsibility:

    • Monitor how government spends public money
    • Report corruption and abuse of power
    • Demand transparency in political decisions
    • Expose human rights violations

    2. Citizen Representation

    Civil society gives voice to groups often unheard:

    • Defend interests of peasants, poor people, women, youth
    • Bring community concerns to authorities
    • Create platforms for citizen participation
    • Mobilize people to protect their rights

    3. Civic Education

    Civil society informs and educates citizens:

    • Teach people about their rights and responsibilities
    • Explain how political system works
    • Encourage democratic participation
    • Combat disinformation

    4. Social Services

    Many civil society organizations offer direct services:

    • Health clinics in areas without access
    • Education and training programs
    • Legal assistance for people without means
    • Emergency relief

    5. Space for Public Debate

    Civil society creates space for democratic discourse:

    • Organize forums and debates on public issues
    • Facilitate dialogue between different groups
    • Promote culture of tolerance and respect
    • Encourage citizen participation in local decisions

    Civil Society in Haiti: Historical Context

    Colonial and Post-Independence Period

    Since colonial times, Haitians have organized in groups for resistance and solidarity. Lakou, secret societies, and vodou organizations played roles in social mobilization.

    Under Duvalier Dictatorship (1957-1986)

    The Duvalier dictatorship crushed independent organizations. The Catholic Church and some religious groups remained one of the few spaces of resistance. Mission radio played a key role in information and mobilization.

    After 1986: Renaissance of Civil Society

    After Duvalier's fall, there was an explosion of civil society organizations:

    • Human rights NGOs multiplied
    • Women's groups and peasant movements organized
    • Professional associations reformed
    • Independent media developed

    Today

    Haiti has a vibrant civil society but confronting major challenges. Insecurity, poverty, and political instability limit organizational capacity.

    Key Civil Society Organizations in Haiti

    Human Rights and Justice

    • RNDDH (National Network for Defense of Human Rights): Network that monitors human rights violations, publishes reports, and defends victims
    • JILAP (Justice and Peace Commission): Church commission working on justice, peace, and human rights
    • Fondasyon 30 Septanm: Organization defending citizen rights and fighting impunity
    • CONHANE (National Council of Haitian Civil Society): Council coordinating multiple organizations' actions

    Women's Rights

    • SOFA (Solidarite Fanm Ayisyen): Fight for women's rights, gender justice, and equality
    • Kay Fanm: Support violence victims and empowerment programs
    • AFASDA: Association defending women's rights in development
    • FEF (Fonds Egalité Femmes) 2025-2026: French Embassy fund financing women's rights projects

    Rural Development and Peasants

    • MPP (Mouvman Peyizan Papay): Movement defending peasant land and sustainable agriculture
    • Tèt Kole Ti Peyizan Ayisyen: Network fighting for food sovereignty
    • MPNKP (National Peasant Movement Kongre Papay): 10 departments, working on pig repopulation, agroecology
    • OPODNE: Organization working with 56 communities in Northeast

    Environment

    • Fondation Seguin: Reforestation and watershed protection
    • CROSE (Center for Socio-Environmental Research and Observation): Research and environmental advocacy
    • Land Reform Groups: Fight for reasonable land management

    Education and Youth

    • FOKAL: Training, civic education, and cultural
    • EDE-RED Groups: Education and youth empowerment
    • FONHDILAC: Foundation for education and community development

    Transitional Presidential Council (CPT) and Civil Society

    CPT Composition

    In March 2024, Haiti established a Transitional Presidential Council with 9 members:

    • 7 voting members: Represent political parties and civil society sectors
    • 2 non-voting observers: Participate in discussions but don't vote

    Civil Society Participation

    Civil society participated in creating the CPT:

    • Negotiation of political agreement leading to CPT
    • Representation of some sectors in the Council
    • Monitoring CPT actions
    • Demanding transparency and accountability

    Challenges and Criticism

    Civil society criticized CPT for:

    • Lack of transparency in decisions
    • Conflicts of interest among members
    • Not consulting ordinary citizens
    • Repeating traditional political errors

    Civil Society Challenges in Haiti

    Insecurity

    Gang violence limits organizational capacity to operate:

    • Human rights activists under threat
    • NGO offices closed due to violence
    • Difficult to reach communities in dangerous areas
    • Activists forced to leave the country

    Lack of Resources

    Many organizations depend on foreign funding:

    • Lack of local sustainability
    • Dependence on international donations
    • Difficulty paying qualified staff
    • Limited equipment and infrastructure

    Political Crisis

    Political instability creates difficult environment:

    • Frequently changing governments don't provide continuity
    • Political priorities don't reflect civil society needs
    • Repression against government critics
    • Lack of space for real dialogue

    Internal Divisions

    Civil society not always united:

    • Competition for funding creates rivalry
    • Ideological differences separate organizations
    • Lack of coordination limits impact
    • Some organizations too close to politics

    Lack of Public Trust

    After decades of corruption, some citizens don't trust NGOs:

    • Perception that NGOs are doing business
    • Accusations of mismanagement or corruption
    • Feel organizations too far from people's reality
    • Challenge to mobilize mass participation

    Civil Society Impact on Social Change

    PetroChallenge Movement (2018-2019)

    Civil society played central role in mobilization against corruption:

    • Published UCREF report on embezzlement of $4 billion
    • Organized demonstrations and general strikes
    • Used hashtag #KotKòbPetwoKaribea to mobilize
    • Put pressure for justice (despite failure so far)

    Defense of Peasant Land Rights

    Peasant organizations fought against agribusiness taking their land:

    • Tèt Kole movement fighting for food sovereignty
    • MPNKP working in 10 departments for pig repopulation and agroecology
    • OPODNE mobilizing 56 communities in Northeast
    • Resistance against projects displacing peasants

    Advancing Women's Rights

    Haitian women's movement achieved important victories:

    • Criminalization of rape in 2005 Penal Code
    • 2013 Law on Common Law Partners
    • 30% women quota in elective positions (though not always applied)
    • Created ministries and institutions for women's rights

    Election Monitoring

    Civil society plays key role in elections:

    • Independent electoral observation
    • Voter education
    • Reporting fraud and irregularities
    • Advocacy for electoral reform

    How to Engage in Civil Society

    1. Join an Organization

    Search for groups working on issues important to you:

    • Human rights, justice, transparency
    • Women's rights, gender equality
    • Environment, sustainable agriculture
    • Education, health, development

    2. Volunteer Your Time

    • Participate in organization activities
    • Offer your skills (design, technology, management)
    • Help in information campaigns
    • Support event logistics

    3. Financial Support

    • Donate even small amounts help
    • Become organization member
    • Buy social products (artisan, cooperatives)
    • Participate in fundraising activities

    4. Share Information

    • Follow organizations on social media
    • Share reports and important information
    • Talk to others about organizations' work
    • Combat disinformation

    5. Create Your Own Organization

    If no organization is working on problems you see:

    • Gather others who share your vision
    • Define clear mission and objectives
    • Start with small-scale actions in your community
    • Seek support and advice from other organizations

    Protection of Civil Society Activists

    Risks They Face

    Human rights activists and journalists in Haiti often under threat:

    • Intimidation and physical attacks
    • Threats to their lives
    • Abusive legal prosecution
    • Defamation campaigns

    Protection Measures

    • Solidarity network between organizations
    • Rapid alert when activists under threat
    • International attention for serious cases
    • Legal and psychological support

    Need for a Law

    Haiti needs a law to protect human rights defenders that would:

    • Recognize activists' important role
    • Guarantee their security
    • Sanction people who attack them
    • Protect corruption whistleblowers

    Future of Civil Society in Haiti

    Opportunities

    • Technology: Social media and digital platforms provide new mobilization tools
    • Diaspora: Haitians abroad can support local organizations' work
    • Youth: New generation of activists with innovative ideas
    • Resilience: Rich history of resistance and solidarity

    Continuing Challenges

    • Need for more local sustainability and less foreign dependence
    • Strengthen coordination between organizations
    • Deepen in rural communities and marginalized areas
    • Rebuild trust between organizations and popular masses

    Priorities for the Future

    • Strengthen local organizational capacity
    • Protect human rights activists
    • Promote transparency in civil society sector
    • Encourage mass citizen participation
    • Build solid alliances for social change

    Conclusion

    Civil society is the backbone of a functioning democracy. In Haiti, despite enormous challenges, civil society organizations continue to defend citizen rights, demand government accountability, and work for a more just society.

    Every citizen can contribute to civil society – whether as member of an organization, volunteer, donor, or simply by sharing information and defending democratic principles.

    Civil society is the people's voice. When we organize together, we are stronger than governments that don't account, criminals who abuse power, and systems that exploit us.
