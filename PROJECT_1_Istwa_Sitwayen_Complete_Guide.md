# 1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

## What Is "Istwa Sitwayen"?

A weekly video interview series where everyday Haitians share their perspectives on citizenship, community, and civic responsibility. Published as 60-90 second clips on TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook.

## Core Concept

**Format:** Person-on-the-street style interviews
**Language:** Haitian Creole (primary), French (occasional)
**Length:** 60-90 seconds per video
**Frequency:** 2-3 videos per week
**Platforms:** TikTok (primary), Instagram Reels, Facebook Reels, YouTube Shorts

## Goals

**Month 1-3 (Foundation):**
- Produce 24-36 videos
- Interview 30-50 different people
- Gain 500-1,000 followers
- Establish posting rhythm
- Build local recognition

**Month 4-6 (Growth):**
- Produce 36-48 videos
- Interview 50-80 people
- Gain 2,000-5,000 followers
- Partner with 2-3 local organizations
- Media coverage (local radio/newspapers)

**Month 7-12 (Impact):**
- Produce 72-96 videos
- Interview 100+ people
- Gain 10,000+ followers
- Create compilation report
- Use as proof-of-concept for funding

## Why This Works

‚úÖ **Zero budget required** (use phone you have)
‚úÖ **Builds real community relationships** (not extractive research)
‚úÖ **Creates research data** (qualitative insights on civic attitudes)
‚úÖ **Demonstrates grassroots engagement** (proves community trust)
‚úÖ **Generates social media presence** (algorithmic advantage on TikTok)
‚úÖ **Culturally authentic** (Haitians speaking for themselves)
‚úÖ **Scalable** (can expand to multiple cities)

## Day 2: Location Scouting

### Best Places to Film Interviews

**High-Traffic Public Spaces:**
- ‚úÖ Market areas (early morning, good light)
- ‚úÖ Bus stations
- ‚úÖ University campuses
- ‚úÖ Parks/public squares
- ‚úÖ Church courtyards (after service, with permission)
- ‚úÖ Community centers
- ‚úÖ Popular street corners

**Criteria for Good Locations:**
1. **Good natural light** (avoid harsh midday sun, shoot morning/late afternoon)
2. **Moderate background noise** (not too loud, but ambient life is good)
3. **Visual interest** (Haiti street life in background)
4. **Safe** (you feel comfortable)
5. **Diverse foot traffic** (different ages, occupations)

**Location Planning:**
- Scout 3-5 locations in your area
- Visit at different times of day
- Note best time for lighting
- Build relationships with local vendors/guards

### Technical Setup Testing

**Filming Checklist:**

**Before Each Interview:**
- [ ] Phone fully charged
- [ ] Enough storage space (delete old photos if needed)
- [ ] Camera lens clean
- [ ] Filming in **landscape mode** (horizontal) for best quality
  - OR vertical for TikTok-native content (test both, see what feels better)
- [ ] Grid lines on (helps with framing)
- [ ] Exposure/focus locked on person's face (tap screen)

**Positioning:**
- [ ] Eye level with subject (not looking up/down at them)
- [ ] Subject fills 2/3 of frame (not too far, not too close)
- [ ] Slight headroom (space above head)
- [ ] Background interesting but not distracting
- [ ] Light source in front of/to side of subject (not behind - backlit is bad)

**Audio:**
- [ ] Stand 3-4 feet away (close enough for good audio)
- [ ] Test in noisy area - can you hear person clearly?
- [ ] If too loud, consider filming at different time
- [ ] Speak loudly and clearly yourself (your questions might be included)

### During Interview (10-15 minutes)

**Filming Technique:**

1. **Position yourself:**
   - Face person directly
   - Phone at their eye level
   - 3-4 feet away (arm's length + a bit more)

2. **Frame the shot:**
   - Person centered or slightly off-center
   - Chest and head in frame (not full body)
   - Interesting background (Haiti street life)
   - Check: Is lighting good on their face?

3. **Ask questions:**
   - You can be heard asking questions (adds authenticity) OR
   - Just film their answers (edit out your voice)
   - Test both styles, see what works

4. **Let them talk:**
   - Don't interrupt mid-thought
   - Nod and react (shows you're listening)
   - If they pause, wait 3 seconds before next question (they might add more)

5. **Get multiple takes:**
   - If answer is good but noisy (car honking), ask them to repeat
   - "Sa te bon anpil! √àske ou ka di l ank√≤?" (That was great! Can you say it again?)

## Video Formats (Mix These)

### Format A: Single Answer Spotlight (60 sec)
**Structure:**
- 0:00-0:05 - Intro card ("Istwa Sitwayen")
- 0:05-0:10 - Lower third (name, location)
- 0:10-0:55 - One powerful answer to one question
- 0:55-1:00 - End card (call to action)

**Best for:**
- Profound/emotional answers
- Complex ideas that need time
- Building individual person's "character"

**Example:**
> Question: "Ki sa Ayiti ka devni si tout moun aji k√≤m bon sitwayen?"
>
> Answer: [45 seconds of someone painting vivid picture of Haiti's potential]

### Format B: Rapid-Fire Responses (60-90 sec)
**Structure:**
- 0:00-0:05 - Intro with question displayed
- 0:05-1:25 - 5-8 people giving short answers (10-15 sec each)
- 1:25-1:30 - End card

**Best for:**
- Simple questions with diverse answers
- Showing community consensus/disagreement
- Variety and energy

**Example:**
> Question displayed: "Ki sa yon bon sitwayen ye pou ou?"
>
> Person 1 (10 sec): "Yon bon sitwayen se yon moun ki vote"
> Person 2 (12 sec): "Se yon moun ki respekte lwa"
> Person 3 (15 sec): "Se yon moun ki ede vwazen li..."
> [etc. - 8 different short answers]

### Format C: Conversation Style (90 sec)
**Structure:**
- 0:00-0:05 - Intro
- 0:05-1:20 - Back-and-forth dialogue (your questions included)
- 1:20-1:30 - End card

**Best for:**
- Building rapport/authenticity
- Showing your facilitation
- More journalistic feel

**Example:**
> You (on camera): "√àske ou vote?"
> Them: "Non, mwen pa vote"
> You: "Poukisa?"
> Them: [explains reasons]
> You: "Ki sa ki ta f√® w vote?"
> Them: [their conditions]

### Format D: Topic Compilation (2-3 min for YouTube)
**Structure:**
- Multiple people discussing same topic
- Longer form, more depth
- Upload to YouTube, then cut best clips for TikTok

**Best for:**
- Deep dives
- Showing range of perspectives
- Creating "pillar content" to chop up
