Who is Haitian?

By Birth (Jus Sanguinis):

According to Article 11 of the Constitution, you are Haitian if:

  • Your mother is Haitian OR your father is Haitian (one parent is enough)
  • You are born in Haiti or abroad – doesn't matter
  • You don't need to apply; you are automatically Haitian

By Land (Jus Soli) – LIMITED:

If you are born in Haiti but your parents are not Haitian, you are not automatically Haitian. Haiti does not fully practice "birthright citizenship."

By Naturalization:

Foreigners can become Haitian when they meet these conditions:

  • Reside in Haiti for five (5) continuous years
  • Own real estate property in Haiti
  • Apply legally for citizenship
  • Accepted by the government

Dual Nationality: 2012 Revolution

Before 2012, the 1987 Constitution prohibited Haitians from having dual nationality. If you took citizenship of another country, you lost your Haitian citizenship.

June 2012 Change:

In June 2012, a historic amendment permitted dual nationality. Now:

  • Haitians can hold citizenship of another country without losing Haitian citizenship
  • The diaspora (Haitians living abroad) can remain Haitian even if they get other passports
  • Children born in Haiti with foreign parents can have dual nationality

This change was a victory for the Haitian diaspora, who fought for this right for many years.

Rights of Haitian Citizens

Political Rights

  • Right to vote: Every citizen 18 or older can vote in elections (Article 52)
  • Right to run: Citizens can run for elective office
  • Right to participate: Engage in political debate, join political parties, protest

Economic Rights

  • Property: Only Haitians can own land in Haiti (with some exceptions)
  • Work: Right to work without discrimination
  • Enterprise: Right to open businesses and operate commerce

Social Rights

  • Education: Right to education (Article 32: "The State guarantees the right to education")
  • Health: Right to access healthcare
  • Security: Right to personal security and legal protection

Civil Rights

  • Freedom of expression: Speak, write, publish without censorship (Article 28)
  • Freedom of religion: Choose your religion or none (Article 30)
  • Freedom of movement: Travel within the country or leave it (Article 41)
  • Privacy: Protection against illegal searches, surveillance, or interference (Article 27)

Responsibilities of Citizens

Citizenship is not just rights – it also has responsibilities. Haitian citizens have duties:

Legal Duties

  • Respect Constitution: Obey the fundamental law of the country
  • Respect laws: Follow society's rules
  • Pay taxes: Contribute to funding public services (if applicable)
  • Mandatory civic service: Article 52.1 requires "mandatory civic service" but it's not well defined

Civic Duties

  • Vote: Participate in elections to choose leaders
  • Inform yourself: Stay current on political and social issues
  • Engage: Contribute to democratic debate
  • Volunteer: Help your community

Social Duties

  • Respect others: Treat everyone with dignity
  • Protect environment: Care for the country's natural resources
  • Promote peace: Work for justice and reconciliation
  • Strengthen democracy: Defend the rights and freedoms of all

Active vs. Passive Citizenship

Passive Citizenship

Many people think citizenship is just:

  • Having a passport
  • Voting once every four years (if that)
  • Paying taxes
  • Not making trouble

This is PASSIVE citizenship – you simply exist as a legal member.

Active Citizenship

ACTIVE citizenship means:

  • You are informed about issues affecting your community
  • You participate in debates and public decisions
  • You hold leaders accountable
  • You organize with others for change
  • You defend the rights of others
  • You volunteer or contribute to the public good

Democracy needs active citizens! Without citizen participation, democracy cannot function properly.

Citizenship Challenges in Haiti

Access to Documents

Many Haitians don't have the legal documents they need to exercise their rights:

  • Birth certificates difficult to obtain in rural areas
  • National ID cards not accessible to everyone
  • Without documents, you can't vote, open bank accounts, or claim land

Language Barrier

Most Haitians speak Creole, but official documents are written in French. This creates a barrier to real participation.

Limited Civic Education

Many citizens don't know their rights or how the political system works. Without civic education, people can't participate effectively.

Lack of Trust

After decades of political instability, corruption, and government failure, many Haitians don't believe in the value of citizenship. They feel their voice doesn't count.

Insecurity

Gang violence and political instability prevent people from exercising their rights. It's difficult to vote, protest, or organize in an unsafe environment.

Haitian Diaspora

More than 2 million Haitians live abroad (United States, Canada, France, Dominican Republic, other countries). Since the 2012 amendment, the diaspora can:

  • Keep their Haitian citizenship even if they have other passports
  • Own property in Haiti
  • Vote in Haitian elections (this right is still under discussion)

The diaspora sends over $3 billion each year to families in Haiti. These contributions are essential to Haiti's economy.

How to Become an Active Citizen

1. Inform Yourself

  • Read Haiti's Constitution
  • Follow local and national news
  • Understand your rights and responsibilities
  • Learn about Haiti's history

2. Participate

  • Register to vote if you haven't already
  • Vote in all elections
  • Participate in community meetings
  • Speak up about issues important to you

3. Organize

  • Join or create community groups
  • Collaborate with other citizens on common issues
  • Use social media to share information
  • Create konbit (collective work) in your neighborhood

4. Demand Accountability

  • Contact your representative with questions
  • Demand transparency in government
  • Report corruption or abuse
  • Support journalists and organizations that expose problems

5. Contribute

  • Volunteer in your community
  • Share your skills with others
  • Help your neighbors
  • Protect your local environment

Citizenship for Young People

Young Haitians are the future of active citizenship. Even if you're not old enough to vote yet, you can:

  • Learn your rights and your country's history
  • Participate in school or community projects
  • Use social media to educate others
  • Organize with other youth on issues affecting us
  • Volunteer at local organizations

Young people represent more than 60% of Haiti's population. Our voice matters!

Conclusion

Citizenship is more than a legal status – it's a commitment to participate in your community, defend your rights and the rights of others, and work for a more just and democratic Haiti.

Every citizen has the power to make a difference. When we all come together – informed, organized, and engaged – we can build a country where everyone can live with dignity.

"A citizen is not just someone who has papers – it's someone who acts for change."

Ki Sa Yon Sitwayen Ye?

Yon sitwayen se yon manm yon peyi ki gen dwa ak responsabilite espesifik. Sitwayente a se lyen legal ant yon moun ak yon nasyon. Li defini ki moun ki fè pati kominote politik la epi ki dwa ak devwa yo genyen.

Gen de konsèp enpòtan pou konprann:

  • Nasyonalite: Estati legal ou kòm manm yon peyi (egzanp: Ayisyen, Amerikèn, Fransè)
  • Sitwayente: Dwa ak responsabilite ou kòm manm aktif sosyete a

Sitwayente Ayisyen nan Konstitisyon 1987

Konstitisyon Ayiti 1987 defini klèman ki moun ki Ayisyen ak ki dwa yo genyen.

Ki Moun Ki Ayisyen?

Pa nesans (Jus Sanguinis):

Selon Atik 11 Konstitisyon an, ou se Ayisyen si:

  • Manman ou se Ayisyen oswa papa ou se Ayisyen (yon sèl paran sibi)
  • Ou fèt Ayiti oswa aletranje – sa pa enpòtan
  • Ou pa bezwen fè okenn aplikasyon; ou otomatikman Ayisyen

Pa tè (Jus Soli) – LIMITE:

Si ou fèt ann Ayiti men paran ou pa Ayisyen, ou pa otomatikman Ayisyen. Ayiti pa pratike "dwa tè" konplètman.

Pa Natiralizasyon:

Etranje ka vin Ayisyen lè yo satisfè kondisyon sa yo:

  • Rete ann Ayiti pou senk (5) ane san entèripsyon
  • Genyen pwopriyete imobilye ann Ayiti
  • Fè aplikasyon legal pou sitwayente
  • Aksepte pa gouvènman an

Doub Nasyonalite: Revolisyon 2012

Avant 2012, Konstitisyon 1987 te entèdi Ayisyen yo genyen doub nasyonalite. Si ou te pran sitwayente yon lòt peyi, ou te pèdi sitwayente Ayisyen ou.

Chanjman Jiyè 2012:

Nan jiyè 2012, yon amendman istorik te pèmèt doub nasyonalite. Kounye a:

  • Ayisyen yo ka genyen sitwayente yon lòt peyi san yo pa pèdi sitwayente Ayisyen
  • Dyaspora a (Ayisyen ki viv aletranje) ka rete Ayisyen menm si yo pran paspo lòt peyi
  • Timoun ki fèt Ayiti men ki gen paran etranje ka genyen doub nasyonalite

Chanjman sa a te yon viktwa pou dyaspora Ayisyen an, ki te batay pou dwa sa a pandan plizyè lane.

Dwa Sitwayen Ayisyen

Dwa Politik

  • Dwa pou vote: Chak sitwayen 18 lane oswa plis ka vote nan eleksyon (Atik 52)
  • Dwa pou kandida: Sitwayen yo ka prezante tèt yo pou pozisyon elektif
  • Dwa pou patisipe: Angaje nan debat politik, jwenn pati politik, manifeste

Dwa Ekonomik

  • Pwopriyete: Sèlman Ayisyen yo ka genyen tè ann Ayiti (sof kèk ekstrep)
  • Travay: Dwa pou travay san diskriminasyon
  • Antrepriz: Dwa pou ouvri biznis ak opere kòmès

Dwa Sosyal

  • Edikasyon: Dwa pou edikasyon (Atik 32: "Leta garanti dwa edikasyon")
  • Sante: Dwa pou aksè swen lasante
  • Sekirite: Dwa pou sekirite pèsonèl ak pwoteksyon legal

Dwa Sivil

  • Libète ekspresyon: Pale, ekri, pibliye san sansou (Atik 28)
  • Libète relijyon: Chwazi relijyon ou oswa pa gen okenn (Atik 30)
  • Libète deplase: Vwayaje nan peyi a oswa kite l (Atik 41)
  • Vi prive: Pwoteksyon kont fouye ilegal, syijan, oswa enterferans (Atik 27)

Responsabilite Sitwayen

Sitwayente pa sèlman dwa – li gen responsabilite tou. Sitwayen Ayisyen yo gen devwa:

Devwa Legal

  • Respekte Konstitisyon: Obeyi lalwa fondamantal peyi a
  • Respekte lwa yo: Swiv règ sosyete a
  • Peye taks: Kontribye nan finansman sèvis piblik (si aplikab)
  • Sèvis sivik obligatwa: Atik 52.1 mande "sèvis sivik obligatwa" men sa pa byen defini

Devwa Sivik

  • Vote: Patisipe nan eleksyon pou chwazi lidè
  • Enfòme tèt ou: Rete okouran de kesyon politik ak sosyal
  • Angaje: Kontribye nan debat demokratik
  • Volontè: Ede kominote ou

Devwa Sosyal

  • Respekte lòt moun: Trete tout moun ak diyite
  • Pwoteje anviwònman: Pran swen richès natirèl peyi a
  • Pwomote lapè: Travay pou jistis ak rekonsilyasyon
  • Ranfòse demokrati: Defann dwa ak libète tout moun

Sitwayente Aktif vs. Pasif

Sitwayente Pasif

Anpil moun panse sitwayente se sèlman:

  • Genyen yon paspo
  • Vote yon fwa nan kat lane (si menm sa)
  • Peye taks
  • Pa fè pwoblèm

Sa se sitwayente PASIF – ou senpleman egziste kòm yon manm legal.

Sitwayente Aktif

Sitwayente AKTIF vle di:

  • Ou enfòme sou kesyon ki afekte kominote ou
  • Ou patisipe nan debat ak desizyon piblik
  • Ou mande lidè yo rann kont
  • Ou òganize ak lòt moun pou chanjman
  • Ou defann dwa lòt moun
  • Ou volontè oswa kontribye nan byen piblik

Demokrasi bezwen sitwayen aktif! San patisipasyon sitwayen, demokrasi pa ka fonksyone kòrèkteman.

Defi Sitwayente ann Ayiti

Aksè a Dokiman

Anpil Ayisyen pa genyen dokiman legal yo bezwen pou egzèse dwa yo:

  • Sètifika nesans difisil pou jwenn nan zòn riral
  • Kat idantite nasyonal pa aksesib pou tout moun
  • San dokiman, ou pa ka vote, ouvè kont bank, oswa reklame tè

Barye Lang

Majorite Ayisyen yo pale Kreyòl, men dokiman ofisyèl yo ekri an Franse. Sa kreye yon barye pou patisipasyon reyèl.

Edikasyon Sivik Limite

Anpil sitwayen pa konnen dwa yo oswa kijan sistèm politik la fonksyone. San edikasyon sivik, moun pa ka patisipe efikasman.

Mank Konfyans

Apre deseni politik, koripsyon, ak echèk gouvènman, anpil Ayisyen pa kwè nan valè sitwayente. Yo santi vwa yo pa konte.

Ensèkirite

Vyolans gang yo ak enstabilite politik anpeche moun egzèse dwa yo. Difisil pou vote, manifeste, oswa òganize nan yon anviwònman ki pa an sekirite.

Dyaspora Ayisyen an

Plis pase 2 milyon Ayisyen viv aletranje (Etazini, Kanada, Frans, Repiblik Dominikèn, lòt peyi). Depi amendman 2012 la, dyaspora a ka:

  • Kenbe sitwayente Ayisyen yo menm si yo gen lòt paspo
  • Genyen pwopriyete ann Ayiti
  • Vote nan eleksyon Ayisyen (dwa sa a toujou an diskisyon)

Dyaspora a voye plis pase 3 milya dola chak lane bay fanmi ann Ayiti. Kontribisyon sa yo esansyèl pou ekonomi Ayiti.

Kijan Devni Yon Sitwayen Aktif

1. Enfòme Tèt Ou

  • Li Konstitisyon Ayiti
  • Swiv nouvèl lokal ak nasyonal
  • Konprann dwa ak responsabilite ou
  • Aprann sou istwa Ayiti

2. Patisipe

  • Enskri pou vote si ou pa ankò
  • Vote nan tout eleksyon
  • Patisipe nan reyinyon kominotè
  • Pale sou kesyon ki enpòtan pou ou

3. Òganize

  • Jwenn oswa kreye gwoup kominotè
  • Kolabore ak lòt sitwayen sou kesyon komen
  • Itilize medya sosyal pou pataje enfòmasyon
  • Kreye konbit (travay kolektif) nan katye ou

4. Mande Responsabilite

  • Kontakte reprezantan ou pou kesyon
  • Mande transpare nan gouvènman
  • Rapòte koripsyon oswa abizay
  • Sipòte jounalis ak òganizasyon ki egzpoze pwoblèm

5. Kontribye

  • Volontè nan kominote ou
  • Pataje konpetans ou ak lòt moun
  • Ede vwazen ou
  • Pwoteje anviwònman lokal ou

Sitwayente pou Jèn Moun

Jèn Ayisyen yo se lavni sitwayente aktif. Menm si ou pa gen laj pou vote ankò, ou ka:

  • Aprann dwa ou ak istwa peyi ou
  • Patisipe nan pwojè lekòl oswa kominotè
  • Itilize medya sosyal pou edike lòt moun
  • Òganize ak lòt jèn sou kesyon ki afekte nou
  • Volontè nan òganizasyon lokal

Jèn moun reprezante plis pase 60% popilasyon Ayiti. Vwa nou enpòtan!

Konklizyon

Sitwayente se plis pase yon estati legal – se yon angajman pou patisipe nan kominote ou, defann dwa ou ak dwa lòt moun, epi travay pou yon Ayiti pi jis ak demokratik.

Chak sitwayen gen pouvwa pou fè diferans. Lè nou tout ansanb – enfòme, òganize, ak angaje – nou ka bati yon peyi kote tout moun ka viv ak diyite.

"Sitwayen se pa sèlman moun ki gen papye – se moun ki aje pou chanjman."
