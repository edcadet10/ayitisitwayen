    • Anti-Corruption Institutions in Haiti

    ULCC (Anti-Corruption Unit)

    ULCC is the official institution with a mission to combat corruption in Haiti. From its creation in 2004 to 2024:

    • It published 87 reports on corruption cases
    • But many reports remain without follow-up – no one is prosecuted
    • ULCC lacks legal power to force justice action
    • It depends heavily on political will for effectiveness

    Recent Case: In May 2025, ULCC exposed an embezzlement scandal of $987,000 in a government ministry. But to this day, no one has been arrested.

    Anti-Corruption Task Force (December 2024)

    In December 2024, the transitional government announced the creation of a new Task Force to combat corruption. Their objectives:

    • Strengthen enforcement of anti-corruption laws
    • Collaborate with ULCC to conduct investigations
    • Prosecute those responsible for corruption in court
    • Recover stolen money

    Challenge: The effectiveness of this Task Force depends on whether it is independent and has the resources and political will to act.

    Forms of Corruption in Haiti

    1. Bribery

    Bribery is when people offer or demand money to do or not do something:

    • Paying police to avoid a ticket
    • Offering money to an official to expedite an application
    • Giving "small money" to pass customs without paying taxes
    • Paying to receive a public service that should be free

    2. Embezzlement

    Embezzlement is when an official steals public money or resources:

    • Ministers taking project money for personal accounts
    • School directors selling state materials to themselves
    • Employees creating ghost workers to steal salaries
    • Officials purchasing equipment that doesn't exist

    3. Nepotism and Favoritism

    Nepotism is when people give jobs or contracts to family or friends rather than qualified people:

    • Appointing family to high positions without competence
    • Giving government contracts to friends' companies without bidding
    • Accepting people to university or jobs because of relationships, not merit

    4. Political Clientelism

    Clientelism is when political leaders distribute benefits to gain or maintain support:

    • Distributing government jobs to party supporters
    • Giving public contracts to companies that finance campaigns
    • Distributing food, roofing, or money for votes
    • Promising benefits for political support

    5. Public Contract Fraud

    Contract fraud is manipulation of the process for government contracts:

    • Inflating contract prices to steal the difference
    • Making contracts without competitive bidding
    • Creating shell companies to receive contracts
    • Not delivering services or work but keeping money

    Impact of Corruption on Haitian Society

    Economic Impact

    • Slowed development: Money that should be used for infrastructure goes into private pockets
    • Reduced investment: Foreign companies don't want to invest in a corrupt country
    • Expensive services: Bribery increases service prices for ordinary citizens
    • Growing inequality: People with connections get everything, others get nothing

    Impact on Health

    • Hospitals lack equipment because money was embezzled
    • Medicines don't reach health centers because they are resold
    • Medical personnel demand money for care that should be free
    • Public health projects fail due to corruption

    Impact on Education

    • Schools lack books because supplies were embezzled
    • Teachers not paid because salaries were stolen
    • Students forced to pay bribes to pass exams
    • Diplomas bought without merit

    Impact on Justice

    • Judges and lawyers demand bribes to render judgment
    • Rich people buy their freedom
    • Poor people stay in prison without trial
    • Corruption criminals never tried

    Impact on Democracy

    • Elections bought and manipulated
    • People lose trust in institutions
    • Citizen participation decreases
    • Corrupt leaders stay in power

    Why Is Corruption Difficult to Combat in Haiti?

    Impunity

    Grand corruption in Haiti lacks consequences. No one is arrested, no one is tried, money never returns. This encourages more corruption.

    Weak Institutions

    Institutions meant to combat corruption lack power, resources, and independence. They cannot force action against powerful people.

    Corrupt Judicial System

    Even the justice system itself is touched by corruption. Judges and lawyers demand bribes, files disappear, witnesses are intimidated.

    Lack of Transparency

    Many government decisions are made in secret. The public doesn't know how public money is spent, who gets contracts, what criteria for decisions.

    Culture of Acceptance

    After decades of corruption, many people come to accept it as "the way things are." People say "that's how Haiti is" and don't resist.

    Lack of Whistleblower Protection

    People who report corruption risk losing their jobs, even their lives. There are no laws protecting whistleblowers.

    Anti-Corruption Legal Framework

    1987 Constitution

    The Constitution establishes principles of transparency and accountability:

    • Article 236: Creates ULCC to combat corruption
    • Article 200: Requires transparency in public financial management
    • Article 159-4: Requires Parliament to monitor government actions

    ULCC Decree (2004)

    This decree created the Anti-Corruption Unit with a mission to:

    • Conduct investigations on corruption cases
    • Publish reports on problems found
    • Recommend justice actions
    • Promote culture of transparency

    Anti-Money Laundering Law (2013)

    Haiti adopted an anti-money laundering law that criminalizes:

    • Hiding the origin of illegal money
    • Transferring criminal money
    • Using financial system to hide corruption

    Implementation Challenges

    Despite these legal frameworks, enforcement remains very weak. Laws exist but are not applied against powerful people.

    How to Combat Corruption: Citizen Action

    1. Report Corruption

    When you see corruption:

    • Report it to ULCC (www.ulcc.gouv.ht or tel: +509 2813-1368)
    • Contact civil society organizations (JILAP, RNDDH, CONHANE)
    • Go to independent media if you are safe
    • Use anonymous platforms if available

    2. Refuse Bribes

    Every time you pay a bribe, you reinforce the system:

    • Ask for receipts for all payments
    • Refuse to pay for services that should be free
    • Report people who demand bribes
    • Support others who refuse

    3. Demand Transparency

    • Ask government to publish how they spend public money
    • Participate in public forums on contracts and projects
    • Watch how your representative votes and spends
    • Support organizations that track public money

    4. Organize in Your Community

    • Create local oversight committees
    • Mobilize to demand justice
    • Educate other citizens about their rights
    • Put pressure on local leaders

    5. Support Investigative Journalists

    • Read and share work of journalists who expose corruption
    • Protect press freedom
    • Give information to journalists if you are safe
    • Defend journalists under attack

    Examples of Success Against Corruption in Other Countries

    Rwanda

    After 1994 genocide, Rwanda established a zero tolerance system for corruption:

    • Now ranks 49 out of 180 (Haiti is 168)
    • Tough laws and merciless enforcement
    • Digital services that reduce bribery
    • High-level political will

    Botswana

    Botswana is one of the least corrupt countries in Africa:

    • Transparent system for public contracts
    • Independent anti-corruption authority with power
    • Protection for whistleblowers
    • Quick justice for corruption cases

    Lessons for Haiti

    Other countries show that corruption can be fought if there is:

    • Real political will
    • Independent institutions with power
    • Impunity eliminated
    • Transparent systems
    • Citizen participation

    Role of Civil Society in Fighting Corruption

    Active Organizations

    • JILAP (Justice and Peace): Monitors transparency and human rights
    • RNDDH (National Network for Defense of Human Rights): Reports on abuse of power
    • CONHANE (National Council of Haitian Civil Society): Coordinates civil society actions
    • Fondasyon 30 Septanm: Defends citizen rights

    Actions They Take

    • Publish reports on corruption
    • Organize demonstrations against impunity
    • Monitor public contracts and spending
    • Train citizens on their rights
    • Demand leader accountability

    Conclusion

    Corruption is one of the biggest brakes on Haiti's development. It steals resources that should be used for health, education, and infrastructure. It destroys citizen trust in democracy.

    But corruption is not a fatality. Other countries have succeeded in fighting it. Haiti can do the same thing if:

    • Citizens refuse to accept corruption as normal
    • Institutions fighting corruption have real power
    • Impunity ends – those responsible are tried and convicted
    • Transparency becomes a rule, not an exception
    • Everyone – citizens, officials, leaders – takes responsibility

    Everyone has a role to play. Refusing bribes, reporting corruption, demanding transparency, and supporting accountability are actions that can change Haiti.

    Ki Sa Koripsyon Ye?

    Koripsyon se lè moun ki nan pouvwa itilize pozisyon yo pou benefis pèsonèl yo olye pou sèvi piblik la. Se abi pouvwa pou enterè prive ki fè domaj tout sosyete a.

    Koripsyon ka gen fòm sa yo:

    • Pòdvin: Ofri oswa aksepte lajan pou influanse yon desizyon
    • Detounman lajan: Vòlè lajan piblik oswa resous gouvènman
    • Nepotis: Bay travay oswa kontra bay fanmi oswa zanmi
    • Kliyantelis: Bay favè pou sipò politik
    • Egztòsyon: Mande lajan pou fè travay ki deja obligatwa
    • Fwòd: Twonpe nan kontra, pwojè, oswa eleksyon

    Koripsyon ann Ayiti: Sitiyasyon Aktyèl

    Endèks Pèsepsyon Koripsyon 2024

    Selon Transparency International, Ayiti gen yon nòt 16 sou 100 nan Endèks Pèsepsyon Koripsyon 2024. Sa vle di:

    • Ayiti klase 168 sou 180 peyi nan mond lan
    • 16/100 vle di nivo koripsyon trè wo (0 = trè koripsyon, 100 = trè pwòp)
    • Ayiti se youn nan peyi ki pi koripsyon nan Amerik la
    • Sitiyasyon an pa amelyore – li rete menm oswa vin pi mal chak ane

    Kesyon PetroCaribe

    Skandal PetroCaribe a se youn nan pi gwo ka koripsyon nan istwa Ayiti:

    • 2008-2018: Ayiti te resevwa plis pase $4 milya dola nan fon PetroCaribe soti nan Venezuela
    • Lajan sa yo te gen pou itilize pou devlopman sosyal ak ekonomik
    • Rapò UCREF 2019 jwenn koripsyon sistematik, detounman, ak move jesyon
    • Milya dola disparèt san yon tras – lopital pa bati, wout pa fè, pwojè pa egziste
